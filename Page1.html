Brief Elder Scrolls History

The Elder scrolls story was created by the writers at Bethesda. The game creation was directed by Todd Howard who later became Executive producer of the Elderscrolls Series.

The Elder Scrolls: Arena 1994

The Elder Scrolls: Arena is the very first installment produced and published by bethesda and introduced into the Elder Scrolls series. The Elder Scrolls: Arena was created in 1994 and produced and designed by Vijay Lakshman and Vijay Kashman Ted Peterson. The Elder Scrolls: Arena was originally going to be a game of Gladiatorial combat. The name "Arena" was turned into a nickname for the world of tamriel because the empire of tamriel was so violent.

The Elder Scrolls: Arena, is a game about fighting through dungeons which is randomly generated upon entering the game. The Elder Scrolls: Arena was first released as a cd-rom or floppy disk 3.5 but was later released on gog.com and is able to be downloaded from Bethesda's website and played using Microsofts ms-dos service.
